The Fresh Connection is a fruit juice manufacturer simulation. Teams of 4 must turn around declining ROI by aligning decisions across all four VP roles. Every decision has cross-functional trade-offs — no role can optimize in isolation.

VP Sales drives revenue through customer contracts and promotions, but every commitment made to customers creates obligations for Operations and Supply Chain. High service levels and aggressive promotions feel like wins — unless production can't support them.

VP Operations controls the physical production floor — bottling lines, warehouse space, maintenance, and workforce. Decisions here determine throughput and reliability, which directly set the ceiling for what Sales can promise and what Supply Chain can buffer.

VP Supply Chain is the connective tissue of the company — balancing inventory cost against availability, managing production scheduling, and absorbing uncertainty from both the supplier side (Purchasing) and the demand side (Sales).

VP Purchasing shapes upstream supply. Supplier quality, lead times, and contract terms ripple into every other role. Low-cost supplier choices often look attractive in isolation but generate hidden costs throughout the value chain.
